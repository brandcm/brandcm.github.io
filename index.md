I'm a postdoctoral scholar in the Bakar Computational Health Sciences
Institute and Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics at University
of California San Francisco. I completed my undergraduate education at
Miami University and received my Bachelor's degree in anthropology,
botany, environmental science, and zoology. My graduate work was
conducted at the University of Oregon, where I received my Master of
Science and PhD in anthropology. I am broadly trained as a biologist and
anthropologist and enjoy working at the intersection of these two
disciplines.

My current research focuses on the evolutionary genomics of humans,
archaics hominins, and non-human primates.
